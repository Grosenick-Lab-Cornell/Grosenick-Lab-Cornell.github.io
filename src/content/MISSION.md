# Mission copy — draft for the PI

The mission is the single most persuasive piece of copy on the site: both funders
and recruits read it first. It should be **significance-first** — stakes, then the
distinctive bet, then what it enables. Below is a primary draft plus shorter
variants and a hero headline. Edit in the PI's own voice before shipping.

---

## Primary mission paragraph (for hero / about)

> Mental illness is one of the largest sources of disability worldwide, yet
> choosing a treatment is still largely trial and error — patients can wait
> months to find a therapy that works, if they find one at all. The Grosenick
> Lab at Weill Cornell Medicine builds **artificial intelligence and
> neuroengineering** tools to change that: methods that read an individual's
> brain and behavioral signals, predict who will respond to which treatment, and
> guide new, personalized interventions. By developing machine learning alongside
> the neurotechnology that produces the data — and testing both in the clinic,
> across depression, treatment-resistant depression, and autism — we work to turn
> psychiatry from one-size-fits-all into **precision care**.

## Shorter version (for a tighter hero)

> Choosing a psychiatric treatment is still mostly trial and error. The Grosenick
> Lab at Weill Cornell Medicine builds AI and neuroengineering tools that read an
> individual's brain and behavior, predict who responds to which treatment, and
> guide personalized care — moving psychiatry from one-size-fits-all toward
> precision medicine.

## One-line tagline (for nav blurb / meta description)

> We build AI and neuroengineering tools to understand psychiatric illness and
> match each patient to the treatment most likely to help them.

## Hero headline options (emphasis = bold/accent)

- AI and neuroengineering to **improve mental health**
- Machine learning and neurotechnology for **precision psychiatry**
- Matching every patient to the **treatment that works**

---

### Why this framing

- **Stakes first** (burden of mental illness; trial-and-error treatment) earns the
  funder's attention and signals you're solving something that matters.
- **The distinctive bet** (ML *with* the neurotech that makes the data, tested in
  the clinic) is what separates this lab from a pure-methods group or a pure-
  clinical group — it's the strongest argument to both audiences.
- **Concrete domains** (depression, TRD, autism) keep it from sounding generic and
  let a recruit picture the actual work.
- Keeps the user's requested core: *AI/ML + neuroscience/neuroengineering →
  better mental-health outcomes.*
