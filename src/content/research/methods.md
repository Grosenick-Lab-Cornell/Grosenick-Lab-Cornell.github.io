Brain and biomedical data are noisy, high-dimensional, and irregularly structured.
We develop new **machine-learning methods** built for them: graph neural networks
that learn over the structure of brain networks, robust and reproducible estimators
of connectivity, and representation-learning tools for comparing neural dynamics
across animals and patients. Methods are released as open-source software.
